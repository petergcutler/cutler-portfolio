On-boarding with the Oculus Platform
Conducting a usability test to evaluate first time user experiences with Oculus Touch and motion-controlled software

  • Quantitative Research

In January 2018, I formed a group with three other graduate students to develop and conduct a usability test. We were interested in testing a product with a complex, novel interface — something that presented different questions and opportunities than traditional web applications. We decided to work with VR, using the Oculus platform.

We were interested in understanding how first-time users would grapple with this new interface — what hurdles they would identify during on-boarding, and how they would adapt and operate a software interface with no prior experience.

VR Applications

At the outset of the project, we decided to focus on two Oculus applications:

  • First Contact — the official on-boarding experience provided by Oculus
  • Tilt Brush — a 3d design application designed for VR

We chose these applications for a few reasons. First, we wanted to test an interface-driven product, rather than a game. I purchased the Rift when it launched, and had seen how 3d design applications were taking advantage of the VR medium. As a team, we explored Tilt Brush together and decided it was a great candidate.

We also determined that we couldn't drop users into a complex application like Tilt Brush without on-boarding. As we used First Contact, we found we were interested in understanding users' reception and comprehension of the application, so we included it in our tests both out of necessity and curiousity.

Heuristic Evaluation

Our professor for the class, Rebecca Destello, recommended that none of our teams seek out clients for this class. This gave us great lattitude to explore based on our own interests (and many other benefits), but it meant that we would have to come up with a way to determine our research objectives independently. With no stakeholders to dictate our direction, we used a heuristic analysis of our applications to find worthwhile avenues to explore.
